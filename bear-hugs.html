Bear Hugs from Mom

What began as one mother’s promise became a family of soldiers, neighbors, children, volunteers, and lifelong bonds.

How It Started

My son was deployed twice to Iraq. During his first deployment, he told me some soldiers received no mail, while mail call for him sounded like “Dutton. Dutton. Dutton.” He took what he needed and shared the rest.

We called my son “Bear,” so the care packages began as Bear Hugs from home. Over time, those hugs extended far beyond my son to the soldiers serving beside him.

The Mission

From 2006–2019, Bear Hugs from Mom supported deployed soldiers serving in Iraq and Afghanistan with care packages, Christmas boxes, Halloween treats, Easter baskets, and reminders of home.

Letters from My Soldiers

These letters are among my most treasured possessions. Again and again, soldiers told me Bear Hugs gave them a little piece of home.

The Bear Hugs Family

Bear Hugs became much bigger than me. Neighborhood children helped load boxes, sew Christmas stockings, and raise money. One young boy dedicated his birthday to Bear Hugs, asking for donations instead of gifts.

Where Are They Now?

Many of the soldiers became family to me. Some still call me “Mom.” I have stayed in touch with them, visited some personally, and visited the gravesites of others.

Honors & Recognition

Bear Hugs from Mom was recognized through letters, newspaper stories, Military Times, Guinness recognition, and military honors including a flag flown in Afghanistan.
