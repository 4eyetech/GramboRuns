My Story: It’s Never Too Late

Every marathon begins long before the starting line. It begins with a decision — a quiet “yes” to something that feels impossible. For me, that decision came later in life than it does for many runners, and it taught me that the clock is never the enemy. Fear is.

Difficult Beginnings

I was raised in poverty and overcame significant childhood adversity. I married young, left school, and survived domestic abuse.

Reinvention

Widowed at age 28 with three children, I returned to school and earned an engineering degree. After marrying Tom and growing our family to five children, I later earned a second engineering degree while raising my family and building a career.

Service

My son was deployed twice to Iraq, and Bear Hugs from Mom began because of something he told me during his first deployment. Some soldiers received nothing at mail call. Meanwhile, mail call for him often sounded like, “Dutton. Dutton. Dutton.”

He would take what he needed and leave the rest for soldiers who had no mail at all. That stayed with me. What started with one platoon grew into Bear Hugs from Mom, a nonprofit dedicated to supporting deployed soldiers serving in Iraq and Afghanistan from 2006–2019.

Discovering Running

I started running at age 64 after decades of walking and fitness. What began as a single 5K turned into a journey I never imagined — one that would eventually lead me to 12 marathons across the world.

Running Today

At 79, I continue to run while managing health challenges and caring for my husband Tom, who lives with Lewy body disease. Every mile reminds me that movement is a gift.

My hope is simple: to inspire others to believe it is never too late to begin, start over, or chase a dream.
