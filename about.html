My Story

How the marathon changed my life

Every marathon begins long before the starting line. It begins with a decision — a quiet "yes" to something that feels impossible. For me, that decision came later in life than it does for many runners, and it taught me that the clock is never the enemy. Fear is.

Difficult Beginnings

I was raised in poverty and overcame significant childhood adversity. I married young, left school, and survived domestic abuse.

Reinvention

Widowed at age 28 with three children, I returned to school and earned an engineering degree. After marrying Tom and growing our family to five children, I later earned a second engineering degree while raising my family and building a career.

Service

My son was deployed twice to Iraq, and Bear Hugs from Mom began because of something he told me during his first deployment.

He shared that during mail call, some soldiers received nothing. Meanwhile, mail call for him often sounded like:

"Dutton. Dutton. Dutton."

He would take what he needed and leave the rest for soldiers who had no mail at all.

That stayed with me.

I promised myself that during his next deployment, those soldiers would not be forgotten. I began sending extra packages during his first deployment, but by his second deployment that promise had grown into something much bigger.

What started with one platoon became Bear Hugs from Mom, a nonprofit dedicated to supporting deployed soldiers serving in Iraq and Afghanistan from 2006–2019.

I sent care packages, Christmas boxes, Halloween treats, Easter baskets, and holiday reminders of home. My goal was never just to send supplies — it was to send comfort, encouragement, and love.

I wanted every soldier to know they mattered, they were remembered, and someone back home cared deeply about them.

Many soldiers later wrote to thank me, sharing how those packages made the holidays feel like home — even while serving in Iraq.

Many of those soldiers became family to me. Some still call me "Mom." I have stayed in touch with them, visited some personally, and visited the gravesites of others.

The love they felt — and the love I felt for them — remains one of the deepest honors of my life.

Discovering Running

I started running at age 64 after decades of walking and fitness. What began as a single 5K became a passion and eventually 12 marathons.

Running Today

At 79, I continue to run while managing health challenges and caring for my husband Tom, who lives with Lewy body disease. My mission is to inspire others to believe it is never too late to begin.

Running gives me strength, peace, and perspective. Every mile reminds me that movement is a gift and that life should be lived fully while we can.

My hope is simple: to inspire others to believe it is never too late to begin, start over, or chase a dream.
